Rainier's Top 10 Games of All Time

10. DRG (Deep Rock Galactic)

Starting off the list is DRG or Deep Rock Galactic. Deep Rock Galactic is a cooperative first-person shooter game where players take on the roles of dwarves working for a mining corporation.
The main goal is to explore and mine dangerous underground caverns filled with valuable resources and hostile alien creatures. The game emphasizes teamwork, as players must work together to complete objectives,
manage limited resources, and fend off threats. It is very fun to play especially when you have friends. The endless possibilities and countless different levels make the playable experience more enjoyable.
It also takes some significant skill to pull off harder levels. With incredible versatility varying from character builds and talents to cosmetics and difficulties, you will never get bored even when you're playing alone.

Pros:

  • Cooperative Gameplay- The game excels in cooperative multiplayer, requiring teamwork and communication to succeed, which can be a lot of fun with friends.
  • Varied Classes and Roles- Each dwarf class has unique abilities, which adds depth to the gameplay and encourages players to develop strategies based on their teamâ€™s composition.
  • Procedural Generation- Levels are procedurally generated, so each mission is different, providing variety and replayability.

Cons:

  1. Repetitive Missions- Despite procedural generation, the core objectives and mission structure can become repetitive over time.
  2. Learning Curve- New players might find the game challenging initially due to its complexity and the need for effective team coordination.
  3. Limited Solo Play- While its designed primarily for multiplayer, playing solo can be less enjoyable and more difficult, as the game is balanced around cooperative play.

9. Subnautica

Subnautica is an open-world survival game set on an alien underwater planet. Players explore the ocean depths, gather resources, and craft equipment to survive and uncover the mysteries of the planet.
The game features a rich, immersive environment with diverse marine life and ecosystems. As players dive deeper, they encounter increasingly dangerous creatures and environments, with the ultimate goal of
discovering the story behind the planet and finding a way to escape. The game combines exploration, resource management, and survival elements, all within a beautifully detailed and vast underwater world.

Pros:

  • Immersive Exploration- The game's vast and visually stunning underwater world offers a deep sense of exploration and discovery, with diverse biomes and unique marine life.
  • Atmospheric Storytelling- Subnautica weaves its narrative through environmental storytelling and in-game discoveries, creating an engaging and mysterious plot that unfolds as you explore.
  • Engaging Survival Mechanics- The game offers a balanced mix of survival elements, such as resource gathering, crafting, and managing your character's needs, which adds depth to the gameplay.

Cons:

  1. Performance Issues- On some platforms, players have reported performance issues, such as lag or glitches, which can detract from the overall experience.
  2. Resource Management is Tedious- Gathering and managing resources is a core part of the gameplay. However, this aspect can become repetitive or tedious over time, especially if you're trying to
    craft specific items that require rare materials.
  3. Overwhelming for New Players- The intense fear factor, especially during encounters with large predators and exploring deep, dark areas, can be overwhelming for new or casual players.
    The constant tension might make it difficult for some players to enjoy the exploration and crafting elements, leading to a less enjoyable experience for those who aren't comfortable with high levels of in-game anxiety.

8. Terraria

Terraria is a 2D sandbox adventure game that combines exploration, crafting, building, and combat. Players start with basic tools and must gather resources to craft better equipment, build structures,
and defend themselves against a variety of enemies. The game features a procedurally generated world with different biomes, bosses, and secrets to discover, offering both single-player and multiplayer modes.

Pros:

  • Endless Creativity- Terraria offers immense creative freedom, allowing players to build and design elaborate structures, and craft a wide range of items and gear.
  • Rich Content- The game is packed with content, including numerous biomes, enemies, bosses, and items, providing hours of exploration and gameplay variety.

Cons:

  1. Repetitive Gameplay- Some players may find the gameplay loop of gathering resources, crafting, and fighting bosses to become repetitive over time.
  2. Pixelated Graphics- The retro, pixelated art style might not appeal to everyone, especially those who prefer more modern or realistic visuals.

7. Lethal Company

Lethal Company is a multiplayer horror game where players work together as a team to explore eerie environments, gather resources, and complete objectives while avoiding deadly threats.
The game emphasizes cooperation and communication, with each player taking on specific roles to survive the dangers that lurk within the game's spooky settings.

Pros:

  • Intense Cooperative Gameplay- The game requires strong teamwork and communication, making it an engaging and thrilling experience when played with friends.
  • Unique Horror Experience- Lethal Company offers a fresh take on the horror genre by combining it with cooperative gameplay, resulting in a tense and immersive atmosphere.
  • Replayability- The game's dynamic environments and unpredictable threats provide a high level of replayability, as each playthrough can offer new challenges and scares.

Cons:

  1. Limited Single-Player Appeal- The game is designed with multiplayer in mind, so playing solo can feel lackluster or incomplete. Without teammates, the game loses much of its cooperative tension and fun.
  2. Repetitive Gameplay Elements- While the game can be engaging at first, the core loop of exploring, gathering, and avoiding threats might become repetitive over time, especially if new content isn't added frequently.

6. Valheim

Valheim is a survival and exploration game set in a procedurally generated, Norse mythology-inspired world. Players take on the role of a fallen Viking warrior tasked with surviving in the harsh wilderness, gathering resources,
crafting tools and shelters, and battling powerful mythical creatures. The game can be played solo or cooperatively with others, and it features a robust building system, a variety of biomes, and a progression system tied to defeating bosses.

Pros:

  • Immersive World- Valheim's richly detailed world, inspired by Norse mythology, offers a captivating environment with diverse biomes and dynamic weather, enhancing the overall atmosphere.
  • Satisfying Progression- The game's progression system, tied to exploration and boss battles, provides a sense of accomplishment as players gradually unlock new abilities, tools, and areas to explore.
  • Cooperative Multiplayer- Valheim is particularly enjoyable with friends, as cooperative gameplay allows players to work together on building, exploring, and fighting, making the experience more engaging and fun.

Cons:

  1. Resource Grinding- Gathering resources for building and crafting can become repetitive and time-consuming, especially when large quantities are required for advanced constructions or items.
  2. High Learning Curve- New players might find the game's mechanics and survival elements challenging to grasp at first, particularly with minimal guidance provided in-game.
